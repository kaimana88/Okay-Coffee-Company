What I Do

Build & Design

Most bars are designed by people who've never worked one during a rush. Looks incredible on the rendering. Collapses by 10am on a Saturday.

Bar layout

Yes, a symmetrical bar is gorgeous. I love one too. But a bar that looks like a spa and operates like an obstacle course isn't doing anyone any favors. Equipment placement, workflow direction, reach zones — where your espresso machine lives relative to your grinder relative to your milk isn't an aesthetic choice. It's a decision your staff pays for in steps, reaches, and crossed paths for the next five years. Function first. Then we make it beautiful. In that order.

Your bar layout is your staff retention plan

A poorly designed bar is exhausting. Not dramatically, not all at once — just daily. A thousand small frustrations that stack up until your best barista quietly finds somewhere else to work. A functional bar removes the friction. Staff who aren't fighting their workspace show up differently. They're present, they're engaged, they take care of guests because they have the space and energy to do it. Happy employees stay longer. Happy employees make happy guests. That's not a philosophy — that's just how it works.

Buildout consultation

I get in before the contractor touches anything. Walls, plumbing rough-ins, electrical drops, counter heights — all of it. Fixing a bad layout after the concrete is poured is expensive and painful. Fixing it on paper costs a conversation.

Equipment selection

What works for your volume, your team, and your menu. Not what's trending, not the flashiest thing on the market, not whatever someone else is getting a referral fee to push on you.

Service flow

Where orders land, where drinks go out, how two people can work the same bar without bumping into each other twenty times a shift. Efficiency isn't cold — it's what gives your staff the breathing room to actually take care of guests.

Build it right behind the bar first. Everything else falls into place.

What I Do

Menu & Program

A menu that nobody can execute consistently isn't a menu. It's a liability with a chalkboard font.

Coffee program

Built around your space, your equipment, and your clientele. Not a copy-paste from somewhere else, not twelve seasonal specials nobody can make under pressure. Drinks that are well-conceived, executable by your team, and consistent enough that the third barista on a Sunday can pull it off as well as the first. That's the standard.

Menu architecture

How many drinks, which drinks, how they're priced, how they're described. A bloated menu overwhelms guests and exhausts staff. A tight menu done well builds confidence on both sides of the counter. Your staff knows it cold. Your guests trust it. Everyone wins.

Wine & cocktail integration

When it makes sense for the space and the concept, done properly. Not bolted on as an afterthought. A thoughtful beverage program that covers morning through evening without feeling like an identity crisis.

Cost control built in from the start

A beautiful menu that hemorrhages money isn't a menu, it's a problem. Recipes are costed, yields are calculated, pricing makes sense. Margins aren't an afterthought — they're part of the design.

A menu your staff can be proud of

A barista who believes in what they're serving sells it differently. They talk about it, they recommend it, they care about executing it well. Give your team a program worth showing up for and they will. Happy staff, happy guests. Still the whole game.

The best menu is the one your team can run perfectly every single day — not the one that looked impressive at the concept meeting.

What I Do

Training

Anyone can hand someone a training manual and call it done. Building a team that actually holds the standard — that's a different thing entirely.

SCA curriculum

Proper foundation. Coffee knowledge, extraction theory, milk science, sensory skills. The building blocks that make everything else make sense. When your staff understands why they're doing something instead of just how, they execute better, troubleshoot faster, and don't fall apart the second something's off.

Latte art & technique

Not just pretty drinks. Consistent technique means a guest can walk in on a Tuesday with a different barista and get the same drink they loved on Saturday. That's what turns first-timers into regulars.

Barista flow

The one nobody teaches. How to actually move behind a bar. How to sequence tasks, read a rush, keep multiple orders in your head, and stay calm when it gets loud. A technically perfect barista who can't flow under pressure is still a bottleneck.

Hospitality — not as an afterthought

Technique is half the job. How your staff makes people feel is the other half. Not scripted, not performative — genuinely warm. That doesn't come from a manual. I build it into training from day one because a beautiful drink served cold gets you nowhere.

Building a team that stays

A well-trained, supported barista who loves their job doesn't leave. High turnover isn't just expensive — it kills consistency, kills culture, and your guests feel it. When your staff feels capable and valued, they show up differently. They take care of guests because they actually want to. Happy employees make happy guests. Happy guests come back. That's the whole game.

Training that ends when I walk out the door isn't training. I build systems and culture that hold — with or without me in the room.

What I Do

Operations

A great opening week means nothing if the wheels fall off by month three. Most programs don't fail at launch. They fail slowly, quietly, after everyone stops paying attention.

Cost control

Food cost, labor cost, waste, yield. The numbers that tell you whether you're actually making money or just staying busy. Most operators don't know their real pour cost, their real labor percentage, or where their margins are bleeding out. I find it, I fix it, and I build systems so you can see it yourself going forward. No surprises.

SOPs and systems

Opening checklists, closing checklists, cleaning schedules, par levels, ordering guides, waste logs, and full SOPs for every position on the floor. The unglamorous stuff that keeps a bar running consistently whether you're there or not. SOPs aren't paperwork — they're how you clone your best day and repeat it every day after. If your operation only works when you're watching, it doesn't work.

Vendor relationships

I bring existing relationships. Coffee roasters, dairy suppliers, equipment service, smallwares — people I've worked with, trust, and know will show up. You don't have to start from scratch figuring out who's reliable and who isn't.

Management support

A well-run program doesn't just make guests happy — it makes your management team's job actually manageable. Clear systems, trained staff, and a bar that runs itself frees up your managers to lead instead of survive. Managers who aren't drowning build better teams. Better teams stay longer. Longer tenure means consistency. Consistency means guests come back. Happy employees, happy guests, better business. Every single time.

The stuff nobody tells you

Permit timelines. Health department prep. Equipment lead times. The things that blindside first-time operators and slow down experienced ones. I've been through it enough times to know where the landmines are.

Operations isn't the exciting part. But it's the part that determines whether everything else was worth building.
